Vela App

Project Summary

The Vela App is designed to provide intuitively designed and easy-to-understand wind, wave, and weather reports, forecasts, and statistics for sailors, surfers, divers, and other water sports aficionados. The purpose of the project was to address common issues faced by these users due to data overload, complex user interfaces, and inaccurate forecasting in existing apps. Vela App aims to solve these problems with data simplification, user-friendly design, community-driven content, and advanced forecasting algorithms.

Context

The context of the project was the growing need for accurate and easily accessible weather and wave data in the field of water sports and activities. The project started in January 2024. The team for this project was led by Kevin Esteves (Lead UX/UI Designer), who undertook this as a solo project.

Tools

During the UX/UI design process for the Vela App, various tools were used to aid in collaboration, design creation, progress tracking, and user behavior analysis. Figma was utilized for creating wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes, InVision for interactive prototypes, Miro for brainstorming sessions and ideation process, and Google Analytics for user behavior analysis.

Overall, these tools played a crucial role in the efficient design process of the Vela App, enabling the team to collaborate effectively, design a user-friendly interface, track progress, and analyze user behavior, while optimizing the app's features and functionality.

Risk / Opportunity

The primary risk revolves around the potential saturation of the weather app market, given the presence of established platforms. These platforms already cater to specific water-based activities, posing a challenge for our app to distinguish itself. However, there is an opportunity to carve a niche in this competitive landscape. While the existing apps dominate in their specialized areas, they might overlook the broader market for users seeking a consolidated, comprehensive weather experience.

Conclusions

Despite the competition, there is a viable opportunity to create a distinctive weather app that caters to a broader audience seeking a comprehensive weather solution. The key lies in delivering a user-friendly interface, ensuring our app stands out amidst the established platforms. A robust marketing strategy will be crucial to gain visibility and attract customers who may find our app more appealing for its comprehensive approach to weather conditions.

The goal of the Vela App project is to create a seamless, visually appealing, and user-friendly experience that aligns with the app's objectives, promotes brand consistency, and supports the needs of water sports enthusiasts.

Exploration & Problem Statement

The problem statement for the Vela App is based on user research conducted through methods such as user interviews and surveys. The key findings from the research reveal that users of existing weather and wave forecast apps feel overwhelmed with the vast amount of data, find the interfaces too complex, and often face inaccurate forecasting. There was also a lack of community-driven content. The team conducted 15 in-depth user interviews and distributed a survey to over 200 water sports enthusiasts.

The research revealed several pain points:

Data Overload

Users felt overwhelmed by the vast amount of data.

Interface Complexity

The interfaces of existing apps were too complex.

Community Content Gap

There was a lack of community-driven content.

Inaccurate Forecasts

Forecasts were often inaccurate.

Design Process

We adopted the design thinking framework for the design process. This involved empathizing with users, defining the problem, ideating, prototyping, and implementing. User research and feedback helped us understand their needs and constraints, and we generated design ideas through brainstorming and collaboration. Prototypes were tested with real users to gather feedback for iterative design improvements.

User Research Data

  • 1. Forecast Accessibility and Preferences: Understand users' methods of obtaining weather forecasts before sailing or surfing, exploring their preferences in forecast presentation. Learn about the preferred channels, platforms, and specific information users seek when planning water activities.
  • 2. User Attitudes and Platform Usage: Identify users' attitudes and behaviors when planning a day for sailing or surfing, including the types of apps or platforms they commonly use. Explore user sentiments, app usage patterns, and the frequency of checking forecasts during an average day, gauging their level of engagement.
  • 3. Forecast Tolerance and Pain Points: Evaluate the threshold for forecast acceptance, understanding the parameters that define an acceptable, no-go, or perfect forecast for users. Determine the factors that influence users' decisions to engage in water activities, uncovering any frustrations or challenges they encounter during the preparation process.

Interviews

During the interviews, participants provided valuable insights into their approach to weather forecasts and outdoor activities. The conversations were insightful, covering preferences, sources of information, and decision-making processes related to weather conditions.

Card Sorting

Out of the 11 participants initially engaged in the card sorting exercise, only 5 successfully completed the study due to the screening question filtering out participants with limited experience or insight into weather forecast apps.

Key Findings

During the interviews, participants provided valuable insights into their approach to weather forecasts and outdoor activities. The conversations were insightful, covering preferences, sources of information, and decision-making processes related to weather conditions.

  • Based on user interviews, participants prefer local insights for weather conditions, relying on various sources including local individuals and online platforms.
  • Crucial factors influencing decisions include forecast accuracy and timeliness. Users value user-friendly interfaces in weather apps and often change plans based on accurate forecasts.
  • They check weather updates frequently during outdoor activities and consult multiple sources for comprehensive information. Despite challenges and frustrations, users prefer up-to-date weather information for informed decisions.

Evaluation

The usability testing sessions were conducted using the Figma prototype on testers' mobile devices. The testing scenario was divided into five subtopics: registration and login, overall app impressions, weather data interpretation, customization of the dashboard, and community engagement. Testers were also invited to complete an SUS questionnaire and share open-ended feedback.
